The State of AI Report Compute Index tracks the size of public, private and national HPC clusters, as well as the utilisation of various AI chips in AI research papers. As a key substrate upon which AI models are trained and run, the size of compute clusters and the popularity of specific chips helps us take a temperature check on the rate of progress of AI systems.

Hopper GPU count (H100 / H200 / GH200) Data cutoff: 1 June 2026

Notes: Deployed NVIDIA Hopper GPUs (H100, H200, and GH200 Grace-Hopper superchips, counted as one Hopper GPU each) by operator, as of 1 June 2026. Public-cloud, private and national-HPC fleets combined. xAI Colossus 1 is owned by xAI/SpaceX and leased to Anthropic from May 2026; the Tesla and Meta figures include H100-equivalent and aggregate clusters. National-HPC counts are exact; large company figures are best available estimates. Sources: operator disclosures, EuroHPC, Top500 (June 2026), SemiAnalysis, Data Center Dynamics.

A100 GPU count Data cutoff: 1 June 2026

Notes: Deployed NVIDIA A100 (Ampere) GPUs by operator, as of 1 June 2026. Ampere is a legacy generation with no new builds; several fleets are being retired. Sources: operator and national-HPC disclosures, Top500.

Blackwell GPU count (B200 / B300) Data cutoff: 1 June 2026

Notes: NVIDIA Blackwell standalone GPUs (B200 and B300 "Blackwell Ultra"), split by Deployed / Installing / Announced, as of 1 June 2026. B100 did not ship at scale; most Blackwell volume ships as GB200/GB300 NVL72 (next chart). Public-cloud GA instance counts (Google, AWS, Azure, Lambda, others) are not disclosed. Sources: operator disclosures, NVIDIA, Data Center Dynamics.

Grace-Blackwell GPU count (GB200 / GB300) Data cutoff: 1 June 2026

Notes: NVIDIA Grace-Blackwell superchips (GB200 and GB300; one NVL72 rack = 72 GPUs), split by Deployed / Installing / Announced, as of 1 June 2026. The Announced segment is committed pipeline, not installed capacity. Counted by owner/operator; tenants (e.g. OpenAI on Oracle/Crusoe) are not added separately. China-bound and export-restricted parts are excluded. Sources: NVIDIA, operator disclosures, SemiAnalysis, Data Center Dynamics, EuroHPC.

Frontier-lab contracted compute (GW) Data cutoff: 1 June 2026

Notes: Frontier-lab contracted compute by silicon vendor, in gigawatts, for GW-disclosed deals only, as of 1 June 2026. OpenAI's AMD ($90B) and Broadcom custom-chip ($350B) deals and Anthropic's AWS Trainium commitment are not GW-disclosed, so the non-NVIDIA share is understated. Sources: company announcements, NVIDIA, Google, CNBC.

Research topic signals by AI chip Last update: June 2025

Notes: The dataset covers 6,356 papers published between January 1 and June 1, 2025 and analysed using Zeta Alpha. Topic labels were assigned using GPT-4o-mini, and we filtered the results to include only chip-topic pairs with at least three papers. To focus the analysis on meaningful differentiation, we excluded topics whose percentage difference fell between -1 and +1 compared to the corpus-wide baseline. The results reveal clear skews between specific chips and research areas.
